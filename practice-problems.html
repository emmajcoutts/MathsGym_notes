Complex Numbers

Chapter 6 Practice Problems

6.1 Solving quadratic equations

Find the solutions of the following quadratic equations.

  1. \(z^2 -4z + 7 = 0\)

  2. \(z^2 + 8 = 0\)

  3. \(z^2 + 24z + 27 = 0\)

  4. \(3z^2 -z + 5 = 0\)

6.2 Argand diagrams

Draw Argand diagrams to represent the following complex numbers.

  1. \(z = 2 + 3i\)

  2. \(z = -3 + 4i\)

  3. \(z = \frac{1}{2} - 3i\)

  4. \(z = -4 - i\)

  5. \(z = 2\)

6.3 Addition

Express the solutions to the following sums in the form \(a + ib\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are real.

  1. \((1 - 2i) + (-2 + 3i)\)

  2. \((4 - 3i) - (6 - 5i)\)

  3. \(3(1 - 2i) + 4(3 + 3i)\)

6.4 Multiplication

Express the solutions to the following products in the form \(a + ib\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are real.

  1. \(i(5 - i)\)

  2. \((2 + 3i)(4 - 5i)\)

  3. \((4 - 9i)(5 - 3i)\)

  4. \((1 - i)(7 + 3i)\)

  5. \((1 + i)(3 - 4i)(2 + 3i)\)

6.5 Complex conjugate and division

  1. Express the complex conjugate \(\overline{z}\) in the form \(a + ib\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are real.

    1. \(z = 7i\)

    2. \(z = 2 - 3i\)

    3. \(z = 6i - 7\)

  2. Using the complex conjugate, express the following quotients in the form \(a + ib\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are real.

    1. \(\frac{3 + 2i}{1 - 5i}\)

    2. \(\frac{2 + 3i}{8 - i}\)

    3. \(\frac{(2 - i)(2 + 5i)}{1 + i}\)

    4. \(\frac{1}{4 - 2i} + \frac{1}{3 + i}\)

    5. \(\frac{1 - i}{2 + i} + \frac{4 - i}{1 - i}\)

    6. \(\frac{11i}{2 + 3i}\)

6.6 Polar co-ordinates

  1. Find the modulus \(|z|\) and principal value of the argument \(-\pi<\text{Arg}(z)\leq\pi\) for each of the following.

    1. \(z = 1 + i\)

    2. \(z = \sqrt{3} - i\)

    3. \(z = -4 - 4\sqrt{3}i\)

    4. \(z = -4 + 4i\)

    5. \(z = -\cos(\frac{\pi}{3}) + i\sin(\frac{\pi}{3})\)

  2. Write the following complex numbers in polar form, using the modulus \(\left|z\right|\) and the principal value of the argument \(-\pi<\text{Arg}(z)\leq\pi\), in each case.

    1. \(z = 2 - \sqrt{12}i\)

    2. \(z = -9\)

    3. \(z = 7 + 7i\)

    4. \(z = -94 + 94i\)

    5. \(z = -3 -3\sqrt{3}i\)

  3. Write the following complex numbers in rectangular form i.e. \(z = a + bi\) where \(a, b\in\mathds{R}\).

    1. \(z = 2\text{cis}\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\)

    2. \(z = 5\text{cis}\left(-\frac{\pi}{3}\right)\)

    3. \(z = 3\sqrt{2}\text{cis}\left(-\frac{3\pi}{4}\right)\)

    4. \(z = 4\sqrt{3}\text{cis}\left(\frac{5\pi}{6}\right)\)

6.7 de Moivre’s theorem

  1. Let \(z_1 = -2 + 2i\) and \(z_2 = 1 - \sqrt{3}i\). Convert \(z_1\) and \(z_2\) to polar form. Then use de Moivre’s theorem along with the rules for multiplication and division in polar form to find the following, leaving your answers in polar form \(z = r\text{cis}\left(\text{Arg}(z)\right)\). You may have to add or subtract multiples of \(2\pi\) from the argument you initially calculate, in order to have \(-\pi < \text{Arg}(z) \leq \pi\).

    1. \(z_1z_2\)

    2. \(z_1^3\)

    3. \(\frac{1}{z_1^3}\)

    4. \(z_1^3z_2^5\)

    5. \(\frac{z_1^8}{z_2^6}\)

  2. Use the polar form and de Moivre’s theorem to simplify the following.

    1. \(z = \frac{\left(2 + 2i\right)^5}{1 - i}\)

    2. \(z = \frac{\left(1 + \sqrt{3}i\right)^2}{\left(2 + 2i\right)^3}\)

    3. \(z = \left(1 + i\right)^{10} + \left(1 - i\right)^{10}\)

    4. \(z = \left(-\sqrt{3} + i\right)^{-5}\)

    5. \(z = \frac{\left(\cos\left(\theta\right) + i\sin\left(\theta\right)\right)^4}{\left(\sin\left(\theta\right) + i\cos\left(\theta\right)\right)^2}\)

6.8 Applications to trigonometric identities

  1. Use de Moivre’s theorem to express \(\sin(3\theta)\) and \(\cos(3\theta)\) in terms of \(\sin(\theta)\) and \(\cos(\theta)\).

  2. Use de Moivre’s theorem to show that \[\begin{aligned} \cos[2](\theta) &= \frac{1}{2}\left(\cos(2\theta) + 1\right),\\ \sin(2\theta) &= 2\sin(\theta)\cos(\theta). \end{aligned}\]

  3. Express \(\sin[5](\theta)\) in terms of sines of integer multiples of \(\theta\). Hence calculate \(\int\sin[5](\theta)\text{d}\theta\).

  4. Express \(\cos[2](\theta)\sin[4](\theta)\) as linear combinations of cosines of integer multiples of \(\theta\).

  5. Use your answer to the last question to compute \(\mathlarger{\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{6}}}\cos[2](\theta)\sin[4](\theta)\text{d}\theta\).

6.9 Roots

  1. Find the following roots and give your answers in the form \(a + bi\) where \(a, b \in \mathds{R}\).

    1. The square roots of \(4i\).

    2. The square roots of \(2 + 2\sqrt{3}i\).

    3. The cube roots of \(-27\).

    4. The cube roots of \(8i\).

    5. The fourth roots of \(-8 - 8\sqrt{3}i\).

    6. The sixth roots of \(729\).
